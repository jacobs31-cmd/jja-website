<p class="lead">Opening a letter that says your insurance company won't be renewing your policy is unsettling — but don't panic, and don't ignore it. A non-renewal is common, it's usually not about you personally, and you have time to fix it. Here's what it means and exactly what to do next.</p>

<h2>Non-renewal vs. cancellation — they're different</h2>
<p>It matters which one you got:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Non-renewal</strong> means the carrier is honoring your current policy through its end date but won't offer a new term. You're covered until then, and you have until that date to line up a replacement.</li>
  <li><strong>Cancellation</strong> means the policy is ending <em>before</em> its term is up, usually for specific reasons like non-payment or a material misrepresentation. Michigan limits when and why a carrier can do this mid-term, and they must give you notice.</li>
</ul>
<p>Either way, the letter will list a date. That date is your deadline to have new coverage in place.</p>

<h2>Why insurers non-renew (it's often not your fault)</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>The carrier pulled back</strong> from a region, a type of property, or a line of business entirely.</li>
  <li><strong>Claims history</strong> — yours, or sometimes the prior owner's on a home.</li>
  <li><strong>Property condition</strong> — an aging roof, an older furnace or electrical panel, or an unfenced pool.</li>
  <li><strong>Underwriting changes</strong> — the carrier tightened its appetite and your policy no longer fits.</li>
</ul>
<p>None of these mean you're uninsurable. They mean <em>that</em> carrier changed its rules — and there are dozens of others.</p>

<h2>What to do — step by step</h2>
<ol>
  <li><strong>Don't wait.</strong> The day you get the notice, start the clock. The worst outcome is a <strong>lapse</strong> in coverage, which can raise every future rate and may violate your mortgage or lease.</li>
  <li><strong>Call an independent agent.</strong> This is exactly what an independent agency is for — we shop 20+ carriers to find one whose underwriting fits your situation.</li>
  <li><strong>Have the details ready.</strong> Your current policy, the non-renewal letter, and any specifics (roof age, claims, etc.). The more we know, the faster we place you.</li>
  <li><strong>Fix what's fixable.</strong> If it's a roof or a specific hazard, sometimes a small repair opens up better options. We'll tell you what actually moves the needle.</li>
  <li><strong>Bind new coverage before the deadline.</strong> We set the new policy to start the moment the old one ends — no gap.</li>
</ol>

<h2>The bottom line</h2>
<p>A non-renewal notice is a deadline, not a dead end. The single most important thing is to avoid a lapse — so act when the letter arrives, not the week it expires. Send us the notice and we'll shop the market and get you re-covered before your current policy ends, usually without you lifting another finger.</p>
