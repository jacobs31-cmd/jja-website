<p class="lead">Between the inspection, the appraisal, and the mountain of paperwork, insurance is easy to leave until the last minute when you're buying a home in Michigan — and that's exactly when it costs you money. Here's a clean checklist so your coverage is right, your closing isn't held up, and you're not overpaying from day one.</p>

<h2>Start your home insurance quote early — here's why</h2>
<p>Your mortgage lender will require proof of <a href="../../personal/home-insurance/">homeowners insurance</a> before they'll close. Leave it to the last few days and you're choosing in a rush. Start shopping as soon as your offer is accepted, and you have time to compare carriers and lock the best rate. A good <a href="../../blog/why-independent-insurance-agents-get-better-rates/">independent agent</a> can quote it across many carriers in one go.</p>

<h2>What your lender requires</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>A homeowners policy</strong> with dwelling coverage at least equal to your loan amount (ideally full replacement cost).</li>
  <li><strong>The lender named</strong> as mortgagee on the policy.</li>
  <li><strong>Proof of coverage (a declarations page)</strong> at or before closing, often with the first year prepaid.</li>
  <li><strong>Flood insurance</strong> if the home is in a FEMA high-risk flood zone — this is non-negotiable for the lender.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Coverages Michigan buyers forget</h2>
<p>The lender's minimum isn't the same as the right coverage. Don't skip these:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Replacement cost</strong> on the dwelling and contents — not actual cash value, which pays depreciated value after a loss.</li>
  <li><strong><a href="../../personal/flood-insurance/">Flood insurance</a></strong> even outside a high-risk zone. Homeowners policies never cover flood, and most Michigan flood claims happen outside mapped zones.</li>
  <li><strong>Sewer / water backup endorsement</strong> — a common Michigan basement claim that a standard policy excludes.</li>
  <li><strong>An <a href="../../personal/umbrella-insurance/">umbrella policy</a></strong> if buying the home pushes your assets past your liability limits.</li>
  <li><strong>Proper valuation of finished basements, decks, and detached structures.</strong></li>
</ul>

<h2>Time it right with your auto — and save</h2>
<p>Buying a home is the perfect moment to <a href="../../personal/auto-insurance/">bundle home and auto</a>. Multi-policy discounts typically run 10–25%, and combining them often offsets a chunk of your new home premium. Have us quote both together so you see the real bundled price.</p>

<h2>Before closing — the quick checklist</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Get a homeowners quote as soon as your offer is accepted.</li>
  <li>Confirm dwelling coverage meets replacement cost (and your lender's requirement).</li>
  <li>Add flood and sewer-backup coverage if appropriate.</li>
  <li>Set the policy's effective date for your closing date.</li>
  <li>Send the declarations page to your lender/title company.</li>
  <li>Bundle your auto and check the combined discount.</li>
  <li>Review liability limits and consider an umbrella.</li>
</ul>

<p>Insurance is one of the few closing costs you actually control — start early, cover the gaps your lender won't ask about, and bundle to save.
