<p class="lead">Michigan's licensed cannabis market is one of the largest in the country, and insurance isn't optional — the state mandates it before you can operate. If you run a dispensary, grow, or processing operation, here's what coverage Michigan requires and what it typically costs in 2026.</p>

<h2>What Michigan requires</h2>
<p>Under the Cannabis Regulatory Agency (CRA) rules, licensed cannabis businesses must carry, at minimum:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Product liability insurance</strong> — at least $100,000 in bodily injury coverage. This protects you if a product you sold is alleged to have caused harm.</li>
  <li><strong>General liability insurance</strong> — at least $100,000 in premises liability, covering slip-and-falls and other on-site incidents.</li>
</ul>
<p>Those are <em>minimums</em> to stay compliant. Most operators carry higher limits and add coverage the state doesn't require but a lender, landlord, or common sense does.</p>

<h2>Typical Michigan cannabis insurance costs</h2>
<p>Ballpark 2026 ranges for a licensed Michigan cannabis business:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>General liability</strong> — averaging around $167 a month, or roughly $2,000 a year, for a cannabis business.</li>
  <li><strong>A commercial package policy</strong> (bundling several coverages) — averaging around $506 a month, or roughly $6,074 a year.</li>
</ul>
<p>Your actual number depends heavily on your license type and operation. A high-traffic retail dispensary is priced very differently from a processor or a grow with no storefront.</p>

<h2>Coverage worth carrying beyond the minimum</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Commercial property</strong> — for your building, build-out, equipment, and (where insurable) inventory. Crop and finished-stock coverage is specialized in this industry.</li>
  <li><strong>Higher product and general liability limits</strong> — the $100k minimums are low for a real claim; most contracts and prudent operators carry more.</li>
  <li><strong>Business interruption</strong> — to replace income if a covered event shuts you down.</li>
  <li><strong>Workers' compensation</strong> — required once you have employees in Michigan, regardless of industry.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What drives your premium</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>License type and operations.</strong> Retail foot traffic, on-site extraction, and manufacturing all change the risk picture.</li>
  <li><strong>Coverage limits and endorsements.</strong> Higher limits and added coverages raise the premium — but usually for good reason.</li>
  <li><strong>Revenue and size.</strong> Most cannabis policies are rated on sales, square footage, or payroll.</li>
  <li><strong>Claims history and security.</strong> A clean record and solid security and compliance practices help your pricing.</li>
</ul>
