Buying a Home in Michigan? Your Insurance Checklist

By Joseph Jacobs·June 12, 2026·7 min read

Between the inspection, the appraisal, and the mountain of paperwork, insurance is easy to leave until the last minute when you're buying a home in Michigan — and that's exactly when it costs you money. Here's a clean checklist so your coverage is right, your closing isn't held up, and you're not overpaying from day one.

Start your home insurance quote early — here's why

Your mortgage lender will require proof of homeowners insurance before they'll close. Leave it to the last few days and you're choosing in a rush. Start shopping as soon as your offer is accepted, and you have time to compare carriers and lock the best rate. A good independent agent can quote it across many carriers in one go.

What your lender requires

  • A homeowners policy with dwelling coverage at least equal to your loan amount (ideally full replacement cost).
  • The lender named as mortgagee on the policy.
  • Proof of coverage (a declarations page) at or before closing, often with the first year prepaid.
  • Flood insurance if the home is in a FEMA high-risk flood zone — this is non-negotiable for the lender.

Coverages Michigan buyers forget

The lender's minimum isn't the same as the right coverage. Don't skip these:

  • Replacement cost on the dwelling and contents — not actual cash value, which pays depreciated value after a loss.
  • Flood insurance even outside a high-risk zone. Homeowners policies never cover flood, and most Michigan flood claims happen outside mapped zones.
  • Sewer / water backup endorsement — a common Michigan basement claim that a standard policy excludes.
  • An umbrella policy if buying the home pushes your assets past your liability limits.
  • Proper valuation of finished basements, decks, and detached structures.

Time it right with your auto — and save

Buying a home is the perfect moment to bundle home and auto. Multi-policy discounts typically run 10–25%, and combining them often offsets a chunk of your new home premium. Have us quote both together so you see the real bundled price.

Before closing — the quick checklist

  • Get a homeowners quote as soon as your offer is accepted.
  • Confirm dwelling coverage meets replacement cost (and your lender's requirement).
  • Add flood and sewer-backup coverage if appropriate.
  • Set the policy's effective date for your closing date.
  • Send the declarations page to your lender/title company.
  • Bundle your auto and check the combined discount.
  • Review liability limits and consider an umbrella.

The bottom line

Insurance is one of the few closing costs you actually control — start early, cover the gaps your lender won't ask about, and bundle to save.
