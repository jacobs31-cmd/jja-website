# Google Business Profile — Optimization Checklist (JJA)

Goal: make the profile match the 45 product pages and stay active, so Google matches you
to more searches and ranks you higher in the local map pack. ~30–45 minutes one-time,
then ~10 min/week. Edit at business.google.com (or the "Your business" panel in Google
Search/Maps while signed in as the owner).

## 3. Weekly Posts  (Prominence/activity lever)

Post **1–2x per week**. An active profile outranks a dormant one. You already make blog
and social content — just repurpose it. Each Post should link to the matching page.

**Cadence idea:** alternate a "What's New / Tip" post (link to a blog post) and an
"Offer / Service spotlight" post (link to a product page). Add a photo to each.

**Four ready-to-paste examples:**

1. **(Tip → blog)** "Michigan storm season is here. Wind, hail, and fallen trees are
   usually covered by your homeowners policy — flooding and sewer backup usually aren't.
   Know the gaps before the next storm. → [link to /blog/does-your-michigan-homeowners-insurance-cover-storm-damage/]"

2. **(Spotlight → product page)** "Run a Michigan business? From general liability and
   workers comp to cannabis, trucking, and cyber, we shop 20+ commercial carriers to find
   your best coverage. Get a quote: (248) 693-6455. → [link to /business/]"

3. **(Spotlight → product page)** "New: dedicated coverage for Michigan cannabis businesses
   — cultivators, processors, dispensaries, and transporters — through cannabis-friendly
   markets. → [link to /business/cannabis-insurance/]"

4. **(Tip → product page)** "Adding a teen driver or buying a boat this summer? One call
   lets us shop 35+ carriers so you don't have to. Call (248) 693-6455 for a free quote."

> Tip: the social posts you already schedule in Buffer can be reused here almost verbatim —
> just add the page link.

## 4. Q&A — seed it yourself  (you can ask AND answer your own questions)

Post these as questions on your profile, then answer them from the owner account. Pull
answers straight from your site's FAQs.

1. **Q: Do you serve clients outside Lake Orion?**
   A: Yes — we're licensed throughout Michigan with clients across Oakland, Macomb,
   Genesee, and Lapeer counties. Most work is done by phone, email, and online.

2. **Q: Why use an independent agency instead of going direct?**
   A: We represent 35+ carriers and shop them every renewal, so we find the best price and
   coverage instead of one company's quote — and we advocate for you at claim time.

3. **Q: What types of insurance do you offer?**
   A: Personal (auto, home, life, umbrella, boat, motorcycle, and more) and commercial
   (general liability, workers comp, commercial auto, plus specialty lines like cannabis,
   trucking, restaurant, and cyber). See jjainsurance.com.

4. **Q: How fast can I get a quote?**
   A: A quick quote takes about 2 minutes online or by phone at (248) 693-6455. Most full
   quotes come back within one business day.

5. **Q: Do you write business and commercial insurance?**
   A: Yes — we shop 20+ commercial carriers for everything from a solo contractor to an
   established company, including hard-to-place lines like cannabis and trucking.

## 5. Photos  (ongoing)

Add real photos: the office exterior/interior, the team, the award plaques (Best of the
Best), and your logo. Profiles with more genuine photos get more views and clicks.
Aim to add a few new photos monthly.

---

## 6. Keep it consistent (Prominence)

- **Respond to every review** (you earn them weekly — a short thank-you reply signals an
  active, engaged business to Google).
- **NAP consistency:** make sure your name, address, and phone are *identical* on the
  website, GBP, Bing Places, Yelp, BBB, and any insurance directories. Inconsistent
  listings quietly drag down local ranking. (Website NAP: 4301 S. Baldwin Rd, Lake Orion,
  MI 48359 · (248) 693-6455.)
- Keep the **review count we show on the site** in sync with reality — tell Claude the
  current Google number and it'll update the site's schema (currently 4.9 / 801).

You can realistically own the pack **around Lake Orion**, but distance keeps you out of the
pack in towns like Troy or Rochester Hills from a single office. Those towns are won with
the **local landing pages** (organic, below the pack) — which is the next site project.
