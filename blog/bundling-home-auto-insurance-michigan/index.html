How Much Do You Really Save Bundling Home and Auto Insurance in Michigan?

By Joseph Jacobs·June 29, 2026·7 min read

"Bundle and save" is the most repeated line in insurance advertising, and for good reason — putting your home and auto with the same company usually does lower your total bill. But the size of the discount is widely misunderstood, and bundling is not automatically the cheapest way to insure both. Here's what the multi-policy discount is actually worth in Michigan and how to tell whether it's the right move for you.

What "bundling" actually means

Bundling — formally a multi-policy discount — means buying two or more policies (most often home and auto) from the same insurance carrier. In return, the carrier shaves a percentage off one or both premiums. It's a retention tool: customers with two policies are far less likely to leave, so the insurer rewards you for the convenience and the loyalty.

Bundling is not the same as buying both policies through the same agent. As an independent agency, we can place your home insurance with one carrier and your auto insurance with another if that combination is cheaper — you still get one point of contact, one team to call, and one renewal review, without being locked into a single company's pricing.

The real savings range

For most Michigan households, bundling home and auto saves somewhere in the range of 10% to 25% on the total premium, with the bigger discount usually landing on the home side. A few things to keep in mind:

  • The discount is a percentage, so the dollar savings scale with your premiums. Bundling matters more in Michigan than in many states simply because Michigan auto premiums are among the highest in the country — a percentage off a big number is real money.
  • The home discount is often larger than the auto discount. Carriers compete hard for homeowners because home policies tend to be more profitable and stickier.
  • Advertised "save up to" figures are best-case numbers. Your actual discount depends on the carrier, your coverage, and your risk profile.

Why bundling usually works in your favor

  • Lower combined premium. The multi-policy credit is real and it compounds with other discounts you already qualify for.
  • Fewer coverage gaps. When one carrier sees your whole picture, it's easier to coordinate liability limits between your home and auto and to add an umbrella policy on top.
  • Simpler claims and billing. One company, one set of deductibles to track, one renewal date to remember.
  • Loyalty stability. Bundled customers often see smaller year-over-year rate swings because the carrier is working to keep the whole relationship.

When bundling is NOT the cheapest option

This is the part the ads leave out. A bundle discount only helps if the carrier offering it is competitive on both lines to begin with. Bundling can lose to split policies when:

  • One line is priced high. A carrier might have a great home rate but an uncompetitive auto rate. A 15% bundle discount on an overpriced auto policy can still cost more than a cheaper standalone auto policy elsewhere.
  • You have a unique risk. A teen driver, a few claims, an older roof, or a non-standard home can push one line into territory where a specialty carrier beats the bundle.
  • Your situation changed. A bundle that was the best deal three years ago may not be today after rate changes. Bundling and then never re-shopping is how people overpay for years.

The only way to know is to compare the bundled quote against the best standalone quotes side by side — which is exactly what an independent agent does in one sitting.

Other discounts that stack with bundling

The multi-policy discount is rarely the only one available. Depending on the carrier, you may also qualify for:

  • Paid-in-full and auto-pay / paperless billing discounts
  • Claims-free and safe-driver credits
  • New or recently updated roof credits on the home side
  • Home security and monitored alarm discounts
  • Telematics / safe-driving programs on auto

A good review makes sure every discount you're entitled to is actually applied — bundles included.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to use the same company for home and auto to get the discount?

Yes — the multi-policy discount requires both policies to be with the same carrier. But you don't have to use the same agent: an independent agency can still shop both lines for you and compare a true bundle against the best split combination.

How much does bundling home and auto save in Michigan?

Typically 10% to 25% off the combined premium, with the larger discount usually landing on the home policy. The exact savings depend on the carrier and your risk profile.

Is bundling always the cheapest option?

No. If a carrier is uncompetitive on one line, a cheaper standalone policy elsewhere can beat the bundled price. Comparing the bundle against split policies side by side is the only way to be sure.

The bottom line

Bundling home and auto in Michigan typically saves 10-25% and almost always simplifies your life, so it's the right starting point for most households. But it's a starting point, not a guarantee of the lowest price. The smart move is to let an independent agent quote the bundle and the best split combination, then pick the cheaper of the two.
