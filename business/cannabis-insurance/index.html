Michigan Cannabis Insurance

Michigan's licensed cannabis businesses face risks standard carriers won't touch. We place coverage through cannabis-specific markets — product liability, general liability, property, crop, and more — so your license, your inventory, and your operation stay protected.

What we do for cannabis clients

  • Cannabis-friendly market access — standard carriers exclude cannabis. We place your coverage through the specialty markets built for this industry, instead of a policy that quietly denies your whole operation.
  • Coverage matched to your license — a cultivator, a dispensary, and a transporter carry very different exposures. We build the program around your specific license type and operations.
  • Compliance support — Michigan's Cannabis Regulatory Agency requires licensees to carry liability insurance. We make sure your policy meets the state requirement as well as your lease and lender terms.
  • Whole-program shopping — general liability, product liability, property, crop, auto, and workers comp, shopped together across the cannabis markets for the right price and the right protection.

Cannabis businesses we write

Cultivators and growers (indoor, outdoor, and greenhouse), processors and manufacturers, provisioning centers and retail dispensaries, secure transporters, safety-compliance testing labs, microbusinesses, and ancillary businesses that don't touch the plant — equipment suppliers, consultants, real estate, and services.

Coverages cannabis operations usually need

  • General liability — third-party bodily injury and property damage claims, including slip-and-falls at a dispensary.
  • Product liability — critical for anything ingested, inhaled, or applied; defends claims that a product caused harm.
  • Property & equipment — buildings, lighting, climate and extraction equipment, and finished inventory.
  • Crop / stock — living plants and harvested product, which a standard property policy won't cover.
  • Commercial auto — for transport and secure delivery operations.
  • Workers compensation — required for nearly every Michigan employer (see our workers comp page).

Common questions

Does standard business insurance cover a cannabis business in Michigan?

No. Standard commercial carriers exclude cannabis activities because marijuana remains federally illegal. A general business policy bought from a standard market will typically deny any cannabis-related claim. Michigan cannabis businesses need coverage written specifically through cannabis-friendly surplus-lines markets, which is what we place.

Is insurance required for a Michigan cannabis license?

Yes. Michigan's Cannabis Regulatory Agency requires licensed cannabis businesses to carry liability insurance to obtain and maintain their license. Your facility lease and any lender or investor may require additional coverage and limits. We make sure your policy satisfies the state requirement as well as your lease and lender terms.

What types of cannabis businesses can you insure?

We place coverage for the full Michigan supply chain: cultivators and growers (indoor, outdoor, and greenhouse), processors and manufacturers, provisioning centers and retail dispensaries, secure transporters, safety compliance testing labs, microbusinesses, and ancillary businesses that don't touch the plant (equipment suppliers, consultants, real estate, and services).

What coverages does a Michigan cannabis business need?

The core stack usually includes general liability, product liability (critical for anything ingested or inhaled), commercial property and equipment coverage, crop or stock coverage for plants and finished inventory, commercial auto for transport, and workers compensation. Cultivators add coverage for lighting, climate systems, and harvested crop; dispensaries add coverage for cash, theft, and assault. We build the program around your license type.

Why use an independent agent for cannabis insurance instead of going direct?

The cannabis insurance market is small, specialized, and changes fast. An independent agent shops your risk across the cannabis-specific markets to find the right coverage at the right price, instead of taking the one quote a single market offers. We also help you avoid the common mistake of buying a standard policy that quietly excludes your entire operation.
