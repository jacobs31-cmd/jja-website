How to Qualify for Preferred Insurance Rates in Michigan

By Joseph Jacobs·July 12, 2026·7 min read

We match Michigan drivers and homeowners to the carrier that rates them best. See if you qualify for a preferred rate →

Preferred insurance rates in Michigan are the pricing insurers reserve for their lowest-risk customers — and the gap between a preferred rate and a standard one can be hundreds of dollars a year on the exact same coverage. The catch is that "preferred" isn't a single national standard; each carrier defines it, and each weighs the qualifying factors differently. Here's what preferred actually means, what it takes to qualify in Michigan, and how to make sure you're landing in the best tier a carrier offers.

The three tiers, simplified: Most insurers sort customers into preferred (lowest risk, best price), standard (average risk and price), and non-standard (higher risk, highest price). The same driver or homeowner can be "preferred" at one carrier and only "standard" at another — which is exactly why comparing carriers matters so much.

What "Preferred" Actually Means to an Insurer

A preferred customer is one the carrier expects to file few or no claims. Insurers build that expectation from your history and your risk characteristics, then price you accordingly. Because each company uses its own model, being turned down for a preferred rate at one insurer tells you very little about what another would offer — the same profile can qualify elsewhere. Landing a preferred rate is partly about your record and partly about being matched to the carrier whose model likes your profile.

How to Qualify for Preferred Auto Rates in Michigan

On the auto side, Michigan works a little differently than most states — which actually puts more weight on the factors you control directly.

  • A clean driving record. This is the single biggest lever. No at-fault accidents and no major violations over the last three to five years is the foundation of any preferred auto rate.
  • A claims-free history. Carriers reward drivers who don't file — even not-at-fault claims can affect how some insurers view you.
  • Continuous coverage. A gap in prior insurance is a red flag. Staying continuously insured, even at minimum limits, keeps you eligible for preferred pricing.
  • A healthy insurance score. Nearly every Michigan auto carrier uses a credit-based insurance score, which draws heavily on your credit history. Keeping your credit in good shape directly supports a preferred rate.
  • Bundling with home or other policies. Multi-policy customers are stickier and lower-risk in a carrier's eyes, and bundling is one of the most reliable ways into a preferred tier.

The Michigan twist — what really changed: Michigan's 2019 auto no-fault reform did bar insurers from setting auto rates on a few specific factors — most clearly your sex, your marital status, and your ZIP code. But the practical effect is narrower than the headlines made it sound. Insurers still price where you live through geocoding and territory, just not the ZIP code itself. Factors like education and occupation can't be the basis of your rate, but they still show up as discounts. And credit never really left the picture: nearly every carrier uses a credit-based insurance score — a separate score that leans heavily on your credit history — to help set your premium. The honest takeaway: a handful of factors are genuinely off the table, but your driving record, claims history, insurance score, and where you live still drive your price. Keeping your record clean and your credit healthy remains the surest path to a preferred auto rate.

How to Qualify for Preferred Home Rates in Michigan

Home insurance is rated differently from auto, and more of the traditional factors still apply. To land a preferred homeowners rate in Michigan, carriers look for:

  • A claims-free history. A record with no recent home claims — especially no water or liability claims — is the strongest signal of a preferred risk.
  • A newer or well-maintained roof. Roof age and condition are among the biggest factors in home pricing. A roof replaced in the last 5–10 years, ideally with impact-resistant materials, helps.
  • Updated systems. Modern wiring, plumbing, and heating reduce claim risk and qualify for better tiers than an older, un-updated home.
  • Protective devices. Monitored alarms, smoke detectors, and water-leak sensors earn credits and signal a well-cared-for home.
  • A solid insurance score. Home carriers in Michigan use a credit-based insurance score that draws heavily on your credit history, so keeping your credit healthy supports a preferred home rate.

How to Move From Standard to Preferred

If you're paying a standard or non-standard rate today, you're not stuck there. The path up usually combines a few moves:

  • Keep your record clean. Time is your friend — accidents, violations, and claims age off, and each year of a clean record improves your standing.
  • Bundle your policies. Putting home and auto with the same carrier often unlocks preferred pricing on both.
  • Fix the small things. Close any coverage gap, add protective devices, and address a known issue like an aging roof.
  • Get matched to the right carrier. This is where an independent agent earns their keep — the same profile is priced differently everywhere, and finding the carrier that rates you best is the fastest route to a preferred rate.

Preferred rates go to low-risk customers who are matched to the right carrier — and both halves of that matter. Frequently Asked Questions

What does "preferred" mean on an insurance policy?

Preferred is an insurer's lowest-risk pricing tier, reserved for customers the carrier expects to file few or no claims. It sits above "standard" (average risk and price) and "non-standard" (higher risk and price). A preferred rate can be hundreds of dollars a year cheaper than a standard one for the same coverage, which is why qualifying for it is worthwhile.

How do I qualify for a preferred auto insurance rate in Michigan?

The core requirements are a clean driving record (no recent at-fault accidents or major violations), a claims-free history, and continuous prior coverage with no gaps. Bundling with home insurance helps too, and because nearly every carrier uses a credit-based insurance score, keeping your credit healthy matters as well. Your driving record and claims history remain the biggest levers you control.

Can I be preferred at one insurer and not another?

Yes, and it's common. Each carrier defines "preferred" with its own model and weighs the qualifying factors differently, so the same driver or homeowner can be preferred at one company and only standard at another. That's the main reason comparing multiple carriers is so valuable — an independent agent can find the one that rates your profile best.

Does credit affect my insurance rate in Michigan?

Yes — on both auto and home, just not in the way people expect. Most Michigan carriers don't rate off your raw credit score; they use a credit-based insurance score, which is a separate score that still draws heavily on your credit history. Michigan's 2019 auto reform limited some rating factors, but insurance scoring remains a significant input on both lines — so keeping your credit healthy genuinely helps you qualify for preferred rates.

How long does it take to move from standard to preferred?

Much of it depends on time — accidents, violations, and claims typically age out of consideration over three to five years, and each clean year improves your standing. But some improvements are immediate: bundling policies, closing a coverage gap, adding protective devices, or simply switching to a carrier whose model already treats your profile as preferred. An agent can tell you which lever helps you fastest.
