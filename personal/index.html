Auto Insurance

Michigan auto insurance covers vehicles, drivers, and passengers against accidents, injury, and theft. Coverage includes Michigan No-Fault PIP (Personal Injury Protection), bodily injury liability, property damage liability, collision, and comprehensive. We help you choose the right PIP tier based on your health insurance situation — a decision worth thousands.

Home Insurance

Homeowners insurance protects your house, belongings, and liability against fires, storms, theft, and lawsuits. A standard policy includes Coverage A (dwelling), B (other structures), C (personal property), D (loss of use), E (personal liability), and F (medical payments). Flood and earthquake require separate coverage.

Condo Insurance

Condo insurance (HO-6) covers the interior of your unit — walls, floors, fixtures, cabinets — plus your belongings and personal liability. The condo association's master policy covers the building's exterior and common areas. Your individual policy fills the gaps the master policy doesn't.

Renters Insurance

Renters insurance (HO-4) protects your personal belongings, liability, and additional living expenses if your rental becomes uninhabitable. Your landlord's policy covers the building but not your stuff. Typical cost: $15-$25 per month for $25,000+ in property coverage plus $100,000+ in liability.

Life Insurance

Life insurance pays a tax-free benefit to your beneficiaries when you die. Term life covers a fixed period (10-30 years) at low cost — best for income replacement during peak earning years. Whole life lasts your entire life and builds cash value, but costs much more per dollar of death benefit.

Umbrella Insurance

An umbrella policy adds $1 million or more in liability protection on top of your auto and home limits. It also covers personal injury offenses (libel, slander, defamation) that auto and home policies exclude. Typical cost: $150-$300/year for $1 million in coverage — one of the best values in personal insurance.

Boat & Jet Ski Insurance

Watercraft policies cover your boat, jet ski, or PWC for physical damage, liability, and on-water medical payments. Michigan-specific exposures include lake-house dock damage, navigational liability, and personal effects on board. Many policies offer lay-up provisions to reduce premium in the off-season.

Motorcycle Insurance

Motorcycle policies have their own structure separate from auto. Coverage includes liability, physical damage (collision/comprehensive), custom parts & equipment (CPE), guest passenger liability, and roadside assistance. Michigan riders should check their lay-up dates carefully before each spring ride.

RV Insurance

RV and motorhome insurance combines auto liability with home-like personal property coverage. Class A, B, and C motorhomes can carry full-time, part-time, or seasonal coverage. Trailers, fifth-wheels, and pop-ups also have specialized policies. We write coverage for everything from weekend campers to full-time RVers.

ATV, side-by-side, and snowmobile coverage protects your off-road vehicles against accidents, theft, and liability. Michigan ATV registration includes minimal coverage on state land but not for private property or trails — a separate policy is strongly recommended.

Common questions

How much auto insurance do I need in Michigan?

Michigan requires minimum bodily injury limits of $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident, $10,000 property damage liability, and Personal Injury Protection (PIP). Most independent agents recommend at least $250,000 / $500,000 limits — and PIP coverage levels depending on your health insurance situation. We walk every client through their Michigan No-Fault PIP options.

Should I bundle home and auto insurance?

Usually, yes. Most carriers offer 10-25% multi-policy discounts when you bundle home and auto with the same company. The exception is when one line of business is heavily discounted at a different carrier — which is exactly why we shop both with multiple companies before recommending a bundle.

How is my home insurance premium calculated?

Premiums are based on your home's replacement cost (not market value), construction type, roof age, location, claims history, coverage limits, and deductibles. Other factors include credit-based insurance score (in most states), distance to a fire hydrant, and risk features like pools or wood-burning stoves.

Do I need umbrella insurance?

If you have assets to protect, kids who drive, a pool, a dog with a bite history, or anything that could lead to a liability lawsuit larger than your underlying auto or home limits — yes. A $1 million umbrella usually costs $200-400/year for most families and is one of the highest-value coverages in personal insurance.
